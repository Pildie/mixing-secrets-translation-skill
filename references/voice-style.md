# Narrative Profile: Mike Senior

### 1. Tone: Didactic, Direct, Confident
- **Source:** "Use EQ in this case."
- **Avoid:** "One might consider that, under certain circumstances, EQ could be appropriate."
- **Action:** Talk directly to the reader like an experienced mentor to an apprentice. Use "você".

### 2. Dry Humor and Subtle British Irony
- Humor must be "functional" (serves the lesson).
- Do not eliminate absurdity. If the author says "chocolate heatsink," the translation must preserve the visual absurdity: "dissipador de calor de chocolate."

### 3. Technical-Accessible Balance
- Follow the sequence: **Intuition → Analogy → Technical Concept → Practical Application.**
- Never introduce the technical term before the contextual explanation provided by the author.

### 4. Conversational Rhythm
- Preserve short sentences at the end of paragraphs (punch lines).
- Do not merge short sentences into long, academic periods.
